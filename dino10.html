Diplodocus - The "Double Beam"

About Diplodocus

Diplodocus, meaning "double beam," was a massive, long-necked herbivorous dinosaur from the Late Jurassic period, around 154 to 152 million years ago. Known for its incredibly long tail and neck, it was one of the longest dinosaurs to ever live, reaching lengths up to 26 meters (85 feet).

Physical Characteristics

Behavior and Lifestyle

Diplodocus was a herbivore that fed primarily on conifers and other plants. It likely used its long neck to reach vegetation high in trees, while its long tail may have been used for balance and communication with other members of its species.

Where to Find

Fossils of Diplodocus have been found in North America, specifically in the Morrison Formation, which contains numerous Jurassic-era dinosaur fossils.
