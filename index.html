CRETACEOUS
The Cretaceous period (145–66 million years ago) was marked by peak dinosaur diversity, with species like T. rex and Triceratops. Flowering plants emerged, and the period ended with an asteroid impact that caused mass extinction.
APOPHIS
ERAS
TRIASSIC
The Triassic era (250–200 million years ago) saw the rise of early dinosaurs like Coelophysis amidst a hot, dry climate on the supercontinent Pangaea. It followed a mass extinction and featured diverse life forms, including early mammals and reptiles.
APOPHIS
ERAS
JURASSIC
The Jurassic period (201–145 million years ago) was a golden age for dinosaurs like Brachiosaurus and Allosaurus, thriving in lush forests as Pangaea broke apart. It also saw the rise of marine reptiles, pterosaurs, and the first birds like Archaeopteryx.

Fossil discoveries uncover the history of ancient life, preserving everything from dinosaur bones to plant imprints and insects in amber. They provide crucial insights into evolution, behavior, and ecosystems, helping scientists piece together Earth's prehistoric past.
