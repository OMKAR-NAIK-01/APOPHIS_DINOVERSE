Apatosaurus - The "Deceptive Lizard"

About Apatosaurus

Apatosaurus, meaning "deceptive lizard," was a long-necked, herbivorous dinosaur that lived during the Late Jurassic period, around 154 to 150 million years ago. It is closely related to other sauropods like Brontosaurus and Diplodocus, and it had a large, muscular body with a long tail and neck.

Physical Characteristics

  • Length of around 22–23 meters (72–75 feet).
  • Weighed between 16 to 22 tons.
  • Long neck with a relatively small head, ideal for reaching vegetation in tall trees.
  • Tail that was likely used for balance and possibly defense.
  • Sturdy, column-like legs for supporting its massive body.

Behavior and Lifestyle

Apatosaurus was a herbivore that likely fed on low-lying plants, such as ferns and cycads. Its long neck allowed it to feed on plants at varying heights, and it likely traveled in herds for safety and social interaction.

Where to Find

Fossils of Apatosaurus have been found primarily in North America, with notable discoveries in the Morrison Formation of the western United States, which is rich in Jurassic-era dinosaur fossils.
