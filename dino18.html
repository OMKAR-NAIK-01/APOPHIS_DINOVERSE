Pachycephalosaurus - The "Thick-Headed Lizard"

About Pachycephalosaurus

Pachycephalosaurus, meaning "thick-headed lizard," lived during the Late Cretaceous period, approximately 70 to 66 million years ago. This dinosaur is famous for its dome-shaped skull, which was up to 25 cm (10 inches) thick and may have been used in headbutting contests or display behaviors.

Physical Characteristics

  • Length of around 4.5 meters (15 feet) and a weight of about 450 kilograms (1,000 pounds).
  • Thick, domed skull composed of solid bone.
  • Small, short forelimbs and strong hind legs for bipedal movement.
  • Small, leaf-shaped teeth suited for herbivorous diets.
  • Possibly covered in scales with ridges or knobs around the head.

Behavior and Lifestyle

Pachycephalosaurus likely fed on a variety of low-lying plants, fruits, and seeds. Its thick skull may have been used in intraspecies combat or as a display feature to attract mates or assert dominance.

Where to Find

Fossils of Pachycephalosaurus have been discovered in North America, particularly in regions like Montana, South Dakota, and Wyoming. These areas were once lush environments during the Late Cretaceous period.
