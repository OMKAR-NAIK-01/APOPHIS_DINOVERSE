Allosaurus - The "Different Lizard"

About Allosaurus

Allosaurus, meaning "different lizard," was one of the most well-known predators of the Late Jurassic period, about 155 to 145 million years ago. It was a fierce carnivorous dinosaur known for its large head, powerful limbs, and sharp teeth.

Physical Characteristics

  • Length of up to 10 meters (33 feet).
  • Weighed around 2 to 4 tons.
  • Large, narrow skull with sharp, serrated teeth.
  • Strong hind legs adapted for running and chasing prey.
  • Short but muscular arms with three-fingered hands and sharp claws.

Behavior and Lifestyle

Allosaurus was a carnivore that primarily hunted large herbivorous dinosaurs such as Stegosaurus and smaller sauropods. It was likely a solitary hunter, though some evidence suggests that it might have worked in packs to take down larger prey.

Where to Find

Fossils of Allosaurus have been discovered mainly in North America, with significant findings in the Morrison Formation. Additional fossils have been found in parts of Europe and Africa, highlighting its wide range during the Late Jurassic.
