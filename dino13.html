Tyrannosaurus Rex - The "Tyrant Lizard King"

About Tyrannosaurus Rex

Tyrannosaurus Rex, often referred to as T. Rex, is one of the most iconic dinosaurs of all time. It lived during the Late Cretaceous period, approximately 68 to 66 million years ago. Known for its massive size, powerful bite, and ferocious reputation, T. Rex was a top predator of its time.

Physical Characteristics

  • Massive body reaching up to 12 meters (40 feet) in length.
  • Weighed between 8 to 14 metric tons, making it one of the heaviest carnivorous dinosaurs.
  • Powerful jaw with over 60 sharp, banana-shaped teeth up to 30 cm (12 inches) long.
  • Short, muscular arms with two clawed fingers.
  • Strong legs built for running at speeds of up to 20 km/h (12 mph).

Behavior and Lifestyle

Tyrannosaurus Rex was a carnivorous predator and scavenger, preying on large herbivores like Triceratops and Edmontosaurus. Its keen sense of smell and binocular vision made it a formidable hunter. Fossil evidence also suggests that T. Rex may have engaged in social behaviors, such as group hunting or scavenging.

Where to Find

Tyrannosaurus Rex fossils have been discovered primarily in North America, including Montana, South Dakota, and Alberta, Canada. Its widespread distribution has provided significant insight into Late Cretaceous ecosystems.
