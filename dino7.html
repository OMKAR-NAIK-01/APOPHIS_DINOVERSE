Brachiosaurus - The "Arm Lizard"

About Brachiosaurus

Brachiosaurus, meaning "arm lizard," was a towering herbivore from the Late Jurassic period, approximately 154â€“150 million years ago. Known for its long neck and front legs that were longer than its hind legs, this dinosaur grazed on treetops, making it one of the most iconic sauropods.

Physical Characteristics

  • Massive body reaching up to 25 meters (82 feet) in length.
  • Long neck used to reach vegetation high above the ground.
  • Front legs longer than hind legs, giving it a distinctive posture.
  • Relatively small head compared to its body size.
  • Weighed around 56 metric tons, making it one of the heaviest land animals.

Behavior and Lifestyle

Brachiosaurus primarily fed on high vegetation, such as conifers and cycads, that other herbivores could not reach. Its long neck and massive size provided a distinct advantage in its Late Jurassic ecosystem.

Where to Find

Fossils of Brachiosaurus have been discovered in North America and Africa, shedding light on its wide distribution and habitat during the Jurassic period.
