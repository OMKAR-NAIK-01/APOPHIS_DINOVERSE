Stegosaurus - The "Roof Lizard"

About Stegosaurus

Stegosaurus, meaning "roof lizard," was a herbivorous dinosaur that roamed during the Late Jurassic period, approximately 155â€“150 million years ago. Known for its distinctive plates along its back and spiked tail, Stegosaurus remains one of the most recognizable dinosaurs.

Physical Characteristics

  • Large, bony plates running along its back, likely used for display or thermoregulation.
  • Spiked tail, known as a "thagomizer," used for defense.
  • Weighed around 5 metric tons and measured up to 9 meters (30 feet) in length.
  • Small head with a beak for cropping vegetation.
  • Relatively short legs, with the hind legs longer than the front legs.

Behavior and Lifestyle

Stegosaurus was a slow-moving herbivore that fed on low-lying plants such as ferns and cycads. Its spiked tail provided a formidable defense against predators like Allosaurus.

Where to Find

Fossils of Stegosaurus have been discovered in North America and Europe, particularly in the Morrison Formation, which has provided rich insights into Late Jurassic ecosystems.
