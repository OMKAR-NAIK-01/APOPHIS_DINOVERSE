Spinosaurus - The "Spined Lizard"

About Spinosaurus

Spinosaurus, meaning "spined lizard," was a massive carnivorous dinosaur from the Cretaceous period, approximately 112 to 93 million years ago. With its iconic sail-like spine and semi-aquatic adaptations, Spinosaurus is considered one of the largest and most unique theropods.

Physical Characteristics

  • Length of up to 15 meters (49 feet), making it one of the largest carnivorous dinosaurs.
  • Weighed approximately 7 to 20 metric tons.
  • Large sail-like structure on its back, likely used for temperature regulation or display.
  • Long, crocodile-like snout with sharp conical teeth for catching fish.
  • Shorter hind limbs and a powerful tail, adapted for swimming.

Behavior and Lifestyle

Spinosaurus was a semi-aquatic predator, primarily feeding on fish and other aquatic animals. Its adaptations, such as webbed feet and a streamlined body, allowed it to thrive in riverine and coastal environments. It also hunted small land animals when necessary.

Where to Find

Fossils of Spinosaurus have been discovered in North Africa, particularly in regions of modern-day Egypt and Morocco. These areas were once lush river ecosystems during the Cretaceous period.
