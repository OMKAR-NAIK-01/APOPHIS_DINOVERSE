Velociraptor - The "Swift Thief"

About Velociraptor

Velociraptor, meaning "swift thief," was a small, carnivorous dinosaur that roamed the Earth during the Late Cretaceous period, approximately 75 to 71 million years ago. Known for its speed, intelligence, and sharp claws, it was a formidable predator despite its small size.

Physical Characteristics

  • Approximately 2 meters (6.6 feet) in length, with a weight of around 15 kilograms (33 pounds).
  • Feathered body, similar to modern birds, providing insulation and aiding in agility.
  • Sharp, curved claws on each foot, including a retractable "killing claw" for slashing prey.
  • Long, stiff tail used for balance while running and maneuvering.
  • Skull with a long snout and sharp teeth for catching and eating small prey.

Behavior and Lifestyle

Velociraptors were likely pack hunters, using their intelligence and agility to coordinate attacks on prey. They were opportunistic predators, feeding on small dinosaurs and other animals, and scavenging when needed.

Where to Find

Fossils of Velociraptors have been discovered in Mongolia and China, particularly in desert-like environments that existed during the Late Cretaceous period.
