Triceratops - The "Three-Horned Face"

About Triceratops

Triceratops, meaning "three-horned face," is one of the most famous herbivorous dinosaurs from the Late Cretaceous period, approximately 68 to 66 million years ago. Its distinctive three horns and large bony frill made it a unique and iconic dinosaur.

Physical Characteristics

  • Large body reaching up to 9 meters (30 feet) in length.
  • Weighed between 6 to 12 metric tons, similar to modern elephants.
  • Three horns: two large horns above the eyes and one smaller horn on the nose.
  • Massive frill at the back of the skull, likely used for defense or display.
  • Beak-like mouth for cutting tough vegetation, supported by strong jaws.

Behavior and Lifestyle

Triceratops was a herbivore, feeding on low-lying plants such as ferns, cycads, and palms. Its horns and frill were likely used for defense against predators like Tyrannosaurus Rex, as well as for mating displays or dominance within its species.

Where to Find

Fossils of Triceratops have been discovered in North America, particularly in the regions of Montana, South Dakota, and Wyoming. It is one of the most commonly found dinosaurs of the Late Cretaceous period.
