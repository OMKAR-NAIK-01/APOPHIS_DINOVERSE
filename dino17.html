Ankylosaurus - The "Fused Lizard"

About Ankylosaurus

Ankylosaurus, meaning "fused lizard," was a heavily armored dinosaur that lived during the Late Cretaceous period, approximately 68 to 66 million years ago. Known for its bony plates and club-like tail, Ankylosaurus was well-equipped to defend itself against predators.

Physical Characteristics

  • Length of up to 8 meters (26 feet) and weight of around 4 to 8 metric tons.
  • Covered in bony plates called osteoderms for protection.
  • Massive tail club used as a defensive weapon.
  • Broad, low-slung body designed for stability and defense.
  • Small head with a beak for cropping low-lying vegetation.

Behavior and Lifestyle

Ankylosaurus was an herbivore that primarily fed on ferns, cycads, and other low-growing plants. Its armored body and tail club made it nearly invulnerable to most predators, allowing it to roam peacefully in its Late Cretaceous environment.

Where to Find

Fossils of Ankylosaurus have been discovered in North America, particularly in regions such as Montana, Wyoming, and Alberta. These areas were once lush floodplains during the Cretaceous period.
